# V1.9.1 Release Notes

**Status:** Production-deployed / GO with an explicit physical paper-scan boundary

**Release date:** 2026-07-20 KST

**Scope:** Optional OMR answer-area layouts, strict generator contracts,
recognition-coordinate preservation, and production real-use closure

## 1. Decision

GO for production use of objective-only, short-answer-only, and mixed OMR
answer sheets. Staff can choose whether an objective-only sheet with at most 40
questions displays the decorative short-answer writing area. The production
generator, upload UI, recognition worker, review/regrade workflow, and student
result projection passed end-to-end verification.

## 2. Business and UX Contract

- Objective-only sheets with 1-40 questions expose `단답형 작성 공간`; staff
  may show or hide the decorative five-row writing area.
- Objective-only sheets with 41-60 questions always hide that area so the
  objective grid has the available page width.
- Short-answer-only sheets support 1-20 questions without an objective grid.
  Mixed sheets keep the actual short-answer area, and an actual short-answer
  count can never be hidden by the decorative-area switch.
- Admin and teacher generators share the same limits and state-reset behavior.
  The layouts were visually checked at 1366px and 1100px.
- The obsolete public four-choice generator was retired. `/omr-sheet` and
  `/omr-sheet.html` now converge on the authenticated canonical OMR generator;
  the compatibility page reflects no query content and loads no third-party
  CDN script.

## 3. Recognition and API Contract

- The new `include_optional_essay_area` field is rendering-only. Recognition
  metadata, contract fingerprints, marker positions, objective bubble
  coordinates, and identifier coordinates are unchanged.
- Request parsing is fail-closed: malformed strings, booleans, lists, floats,
  unsupported choice counts, and out-of-range objective/short-answer counts
  return HTTP 400 instead of being silently clamped or raising a server error.
- Permanent recognition tests cover objective-count boundaries
  1/2/19/20/21/22/39/40/41/42/59/60, a hidden-area 30-question sheet, and a
  20-question short-answer-only sheet. Clean renders, 200dpi JPEG quality 70
  with rotation/noise, and 150dpi scanner-like renders preserved all 30
  objective answers and all eight identifier digits.
- No database migration is included.

## 5. Physical Scan Boundary

The production proof used a digitally marked production PDF through the real
browser upload, worker, database, review, grading, and student UI path. That
vector-PDF canary displayed a page-alignment advisory while still recognizing
all 30 answers and all eight identifier digits exactly. Scanner-like local
tests at 150/200dpi, JPEG quality 70, rotation, and noise also passed, but this
workstation has no physical printer, pen, or hardware scanner. A paper print,
hand marking, and device scan therefore remain an explicit external acceptance
check rather than an unverified software claim.

## 6. Final State

The requested layout choices are live in production, recognition coordinates
remain sealed, and no release-scoped Critical or High software finding remains.
The only unproven surface is the physical hardware loop described above.
