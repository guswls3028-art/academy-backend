teacher finish the educational judgment." Beta rewrite remains opt-in.

## 5. Product Review

The sellable v1 product is now clear: "교재 원본 이관 + 검수 가능한 편집 초안".
That is valuable even before full AI rewriting because it removes repetitive
copy/paste work, carries images/tables/figures into editable drafts, and gives
teachers an explicit checklist/report before they touch the output.

The customer-facing CTA should stay simple: upload files, save review package,
open checklist first. The beta rewrite should remain a secondary accelerator
for candidate ideas until OCR, template fidelity, and teacher approval controls
are stronger.

Next best investments:

- OCR for scanned PDFs/images so beta can read raster-only sources.
- Native HWPX or stricter academy-template output for better Korean document
  fidelity.
- Selective export/page preview for large publisher packages.
- A teacher feedback loop that captures time saved, manual correction points,
  and must-have template changes after the first real class-material trial.

## 6. Residual Risk

- Current editable output is Hangul/Word-compatible `.doc` HTML, not native
  binary `.hwp`.
- Scanned PDFs/images transfer visually but do not feed beta text extraction
  until OCR is added.
- Beta rewrite remains a teacher-reviewed candidate generator, not final
  classroom material.
- Backend CI noted the existing missing `E2E_STUDENT_USER/PASS` optional
  student-chain secret; the job still concluded success, and frontend
  production E2E/login gates passed.
- AI worker refresh was skipped because the AI ASG is intentionally scaled to
  zero; post-deploy canary verified that state as healthy.
